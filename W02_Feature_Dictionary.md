# Aido Rover Sensor Data Feature Dictionary (W02_Feature_Dictionary)

| Feature ID | Name | Formula | Physical Rationale | Direction of Effect on FAULT Prediction |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Recency (Time Since Last Fault)** | $T_{current} - T_{last\_FAULT}$ | Evaluates the cumulative operating time since the last fault. Shorter time indicates system instability. | Negative (Smaller value = higher fault probability) |
| **2** | **Frequency (Alert Count)** | $\sum_{t=current-7d}^{current} \mathbb{I}(Mode_t == ALERT)$ | Reflects the frequency of anomaly alerts triggered in the past 7 days. Higher frequency indicates more potential risks. | Positive (Larger value = higher fault probability) |
| **3** | **Monetary (Weekly Energy Consumption Integral)** | $\int_{week} -\Delta SoC \,dt$ | Calculates cumulative electrical energy consumed per unit per week (integral of SoC drop). Abnormal consumption increases typically indicate mechanical friction or electrical short circuits. | Positive (Larger value = higher fault probability) |
| **4** | **Motor_Current_RollMean_5** | $\frac{1}{5}\sum_{i=0}^{4} Current_{t-i}$ | 5-window rolling mean of motor current. Reflects recent average load. Consistently high mean indicates motor overload. | Positive (Larger value = higher fault probability) |
| **5** | **Motor_Current_RollStd_5** | $\sqrt{\frac{1}{5}\sum_{i=0}^{4} (Current_{t-i} - \mu)^2}$ | 5-window rolling standard deviation of motor current. Reflects current fluctuation intensity. Abnormal jitter is usually caused by mechanical jamming. | Positive (Larger value = higher fault probability) |
| **6** | **Battery_SoC_RollMean_5** | $\frac{1}{5}\sum_{i=0}^{4} SoC_{t-i}$ | 5-window rolling mean of remaining battery SoC. Operating at low battery levels increases the risk of unstable system power supply. | Negative (Smaller value = higher fault probability) |
| **7** | **Battery_SoC_RollStd_5** | $\sqrt{\frac{1}{5}\sum_{i=0}^{4} (SoC_{t-i} - \mu)^2}$ | 5-window rolling standard deviation of battery SoC. A sudden surge indicates false battery capacity readings or instantaneous high-current discharge. | Positive (Larger value = higher fault probability) |
| **8** | **IMU_X_RollStd_5** | $\sqrt{\frac{1}{5}\sum_{i=0}^{4} (IMU_{X, t-i} - \mu)^2}$ | 5-window rolling standard deviation of IMU X-axis (longitudinal). Higher values indicate abnormal and severe vibration in the forward direction. | Positive (Larger value = higher fault probability) |
| **9** | **IMU_Y_RollStd_5** | $\sqrt{\frac{1}{5}\sum_{i=0}^{4} (IMU_{Y, t-i} - \mu)^2}$ | 5-window rolling standard deviation of IMU Y-axis (lateral). Higher values indicate severe lateral shaking, possibly due to rough terrain or slipping. | Positive (Larger value = higher fault probability) |
| **10** | **IMU_Z_RollStd_5** | $\sqrt{\frac{1}{5}\sum_{i=0}^{4} (IMU_{Z, t-i} - \mu)^2}$ | 5-window rolling standard deviation of IMU Z-axis (vertical). Higher values indicate severe vertical bumping, easily causing hardware loosening. | Positive (Larger value = higher fault probability) |
| **11** | **RSSI_RollMean_5** | $\frac{1}{5}\sum_{i=0}^{4} RSSI_{t-i}$ | 5-window rolling mean of network signal strength. Prolonged low signal leads to communication interruption and triggers loss-of-control faults. | Negative (Smaller value = higher fault probability) |
| **12** | **Cross_Unit_Z_Current** | $\frac{Current_{i,t} - \mu_{fleet,t}}{\sigma_{fleet,t}}$ | Cross-fleet unit current Z-score. Compares current robot's current against fleet mean to identify deviating abnormal units. | Positive (Larger absolute value = higher fault probability) |
| **13** | **IMU_Magnitude** | $\sqrt{IMU_{X}^2 + IMU_{Y}^2 + IMU_{Z}^2}$ | Calculates total acceleration magnitude across three axes to comprehensively evaluate overall physical impact and vibration intensity. | Positive (Larger value = higher fault probability) |
| **14** | **Current_SoC_Ratio** | $\frac{Current_{motor, t}}{SoC_t + \epsilon}$ | Ratio of current to remaining battery SoC ($\epsilon$ prevents zero division). When power is low and current is high, battery internal resistance increases, highly likely triggering undervoltage faults. | Positive (Larger value = higher fault probability) |
| **15** | **Proximity_RollMean_5** | $\frac{1}{5}\sum_{i=0}^{4} Proximity_{t-i}$ | 5-window rolling mean of proximity sensor distance. Consistently minimal values indicate prolonged extreme proximity to obstacles, resulting in high collision risk. | Negative (Smaller value = higher fault probability) |
